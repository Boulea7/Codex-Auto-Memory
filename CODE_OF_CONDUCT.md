# Code of Conduct

## Our standard

We want this repository to be a practical, respectful place for maintainers, contributors, and users. Participation here means treating other people with professionalism, patience, and respect.

## Expected behavior

- be respectful in technical disagreement
- focus on the problem, not the person
- give actionable, evidence-based feedback
- assume good intent, but stay honest about risk and correctness
- keep discussion relevant to the project

## Unacceptable behavior

- harassment, threats, or personal attacks
- discriminatory or demeaning language
- doxxing or sharing private information without permission
- spam, trolling, or deliberately disruptive behavior
- publishing exploit details publicly when a private security route is more appropriate

## Scope

This applies to issues, pull requests, code review, discussions around releases, and other project spaces managed through this repository.

## Reporting

If conduct becomes a problem, use the support route in [SUPPORT.md](./SUPPORT.md) to open a minimal maintainer-facing report or ask for follow-up without escalating the public thread. Keep the report factual and avoid posting personal or sensitive details in public. If the concern is security-sensitive, use the process in [SECURITY.md](./SECURITY.md) instead.

## Enforcement

Project maintainers may edit, hide, lock, or remove content, and may limit participation when behavior breaks this code of conduct.
